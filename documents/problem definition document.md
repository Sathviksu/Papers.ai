# Problem Definition Document (PDD)

## 1. Problem Statement
Users struggle to quickly extract key insights (such as flowcharts, methodologies, and tools) from lengthy and complex research papers. Manual analysis is time-consuming, error-prone, and requires domain expertise, limiting accessibility and efficiency.

---

## 2. Problem Description
In today’s academic and research environment, students, researchers, and professionals are required to read and analyze a large number of research papers regularly. These papers are often lengthy, complex, and written in technical language, making them difficult to understand quickly.

The primary problem faced by users is the inability to efficiently extract important information such as methodologies, tools used, results, and conclusions. This process is currently manual, making it time-consuming and prone to errors. It also requires significant domain knowledge, limiting accessibility for beginners and interdisciplinary researchers.

Typical users include PhD scholars, postgraduate students, and R&D professionals who are under constant pressure to review multiple papers in a short time. These users often feel overwhelmed due to the volume of content and lack of structured tools to simplify the process.

Based on discussions and understanding of user needs, it is evident that users are willing to adopt and potentially pay for a solution that can save time, provide structured insights, improve accuracy, and simplify research workflows. This validates that the problem is real and significant.

Additionally, with the increasing number of research publications, users often struggle to quickly identify the most relevant information, reducing productivity and making efficient knowledge management more challenging.
